Who We Are

Work2Cash is a Nigeria-focused task marketplace operated by Traceworka. In these Terms, "Work2Cash", "we", "us", and "our" refer to the Work2Cash platform and its operator. "User", "you", and "your" refer to any person who creates an account, posts a task, performs a task, contacts support, or otherwise uses the platform.

Acceptance of These Terms

By creating an account, using the app, posting a task, accepting a task, making payment, requesting withdrawal, or using any Work2Cash service, you agree to these Terms and the related policies published on our legal pages. If you do not agree, you must not use Work2Cash.

Eligibility

You must be at least 18 years old and capable of entering into a binding agreement. You must provide accurate information, keep your account secure, and use Work2Cash only for lawful purposes. Taskers must complete the required Tasker profile and KYC approval before they can accept tasks.

Account Modes

Work2Cash uses one account for both hiring and earning. A user may operate in Task Owner mode to post and fund tasks, and in Tasker mode to earn from tasks after KYC approval. The mode shown in the app controls the experience and actions available at that time.

Nigeria Service Boundary

Work2Cash is designed for task sites in Nigeria. A Task Owner may be physically outside Nigeria, but the task site must be in Nigeria. A Tasker must be in Nigeria to accept a task. Work2Cash may restrict service temporarily by state, city, or custom service zone for safety, compliance, or operational reasons.

Marketplace Role

Work2Cash provides a platform for Task Owners and Taskers to connect, communicate, fund tasks, track progress, and resolve issues. Taskers are independent service providers and are not employees of Work2Cash. Work2Cash may facilitate payments, escrow, safety checks, support, and dispute review, but the Task Owner and Tasker remain responsible for their own conduct and commitments.

Approved Task Categories Only

Task Owners may only select from approved task categories and task types shown in the app. Custom categories are not available in the MVP. Work2Cash may enable, disable, reorder, review, or remove categories and task media to protect users and platform quality.

Task Posting Requirements

When posting a task, the Task Owner must describe the work clearly, select the correct approved task type, provide the task site, confirm the map pin when required, set the required arrival time, upload proof of work to be done, review pricing and fees, and fund the task before matching or direct offer acceptance.

Tasker Acceptance and Status Updates

A Tasker must confirm they can arrive on or before the Task Owner's required arrival time before accepting a task. During execution, status updates must be made inside the app. The Tasker taps Start Journey to move to en route, taps I have arrived after reaching the task site, and taps Begin Task to start the task. These updates affect task visibility, live tracking, cancellation rules, and dispute evidence.

Task Owner Review of Tasker

Task Owners may review a Tasker's profile and ETA before accepting. A Task Owner may reject a Tasker before the Tasker starts moving and within 5 minutes after the Tasker marks en route. Repeated movement-stage rejection may trigger warnings, trust-score changes, risk review, or other platform action.

Payments and Escrow

Task Owners must pay into escrow before matching or direct offer acceptance. Work2Cash may use Paystack, Moniepoint, or other approved payment rails for collections and transfers. Payment confirmation must come from Work2Cash backend verification, provider webhook, or reconciliation, not only from a frontend redirect or user screenshot.

Fees and Pricing

Work2Cash may charge platform, service, payment-processing, cancellation, or other disclosed fees. Task Owners will see the task amount, applicable fees, and final payable total before payment. Taskers will see gross task amount, platform commission, and net earning where applicable.

Wallets and Withdrawals

A user may have separate Task Owner and Tasker wallet purposes. Task Owner wallet funds are mainly for task funding, refunds, and credits and cannot be directly withdrawn by default without support review. Tasker wallet earnings may be withdrawn subject to minimum withdrawal amount, eligibility, dispute holds, policy checks, provider limits, and payout calendar rules.

Tasker Payout Calendar

Eligible Tasker withdrawal requests are processed in bulk payout batches on the 14th and 28th of every month where Paystack, Moniepoint, or another approved transfer rail is available. A withdrawal request may be held if the Tasker has an active dispute, active issue, risk flag, failed KYC/payment review, invalid bank details, or other unresolved settlement concern. If a payout date falls on a non-working day or there is provider downtime, Work2Cash may process on the next operational processing window.

Communication Rules

Task communication must stay inside Work2Cash whenever possible. Chat, voice messages, masked calls, support live chat, and task reports may be used according to task state and platform rules. Users must not use communication tools to harass, threaten, mislead, solicit off-platform payment, or bypass Work2Cash protections.

Masked Calls

Work2Cash may provide task-bound masked calls through a proxy number. Real phone numbers must not be exposed by Work2Cash during masked calls. Calls use the caller's normal airtime or network charges, while Work2Cash may pay provider-side masking, forwarding, or session costs. Masked call availability depends on provider support, task state, and operational reliability.

Task Proof, Media, and Evidence

Task Owners must upload proof of work to be done. Taskers must upload proof of work done when requesting completion. Supported media may include photos and videos up to the stated file-size limit. Users must not upload illegal, unsafe, misleading, unrelated, or privacy-invasive media. Work2Cash may review, restrict, remove, or preserve media as part of safety, moderation, dispute, or legal processes.

Completion, Ratings, and Favorites

A task is completed when the required completion flow is satisfied by Task Owner confirmation or platform/admin resolution. Task Owners and Taskers may rate each other where available. A Task Owner may favorite a Tasker after a completed and rated task, and may later send direct offers to favorite Taskers.

Cancellations, Refunds, and Penalties

Cancellation, refund, warning, strike, and penalty rules are governed by the Cancellation & Refund Policy. Work2Cash may apply penalties, warnings, strike restrictions, refund holds, or admin review depending on task state, evidence, and user conduct.

Reports, Disputes, and Support

Users may report issues through structured task-linked reports, evidence upload, and support channels. Support live chat is for operational help. Disputes are structured evidence-based reviews and do not use live chat as the dispute record. Work2Cash may request additional evidence, review communication logs, review task proof, and decide whether to release escrow, refund, partially refund, warn, strike, or suspend a user.

Prohibited Conduct and Tasks

Users must not post, accept, request, perform, or facilitate illegal activities, adult or sexual services, weapons-related tasks, intimidation or violence, drug-related activity, high-risk medical tasks, licensed professional work unless specifically approved, overnight stay tasks, unsafe access tasks, tasks involving minors without appropriate guardian presence, off-platform payment or booking, or any task outside approved categories.

Suspension and Enforcement

Work2Cash may warn, restrict, suspend, deactivate, or block an account where there is suspected or confirmed fraud, safety risk, off-platform abuse, payment abuse, KYC concern, harassment, repeated cancellation abuse, prohibited tasks, or violation of these Terms. High-risk decisions may be reviewed by support, risk, finance, or admin teams.

Platform Availability

Work2Cash aims to keep the platform reliable, but we do not guarantee uninterrupted availability. Provider outages, network problems, payment delays, maintenance, device issues, map provider limits, or force majeure events may affect service.

Limitation of Liability

To the extent permitted by applicable law, Work2Cash is not liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, punitive, or lost-profit damages arising from platform use, user conduct, third-party provider failure, task quality, network interruptions, or payment delays. Nothing in these Terms excludes liability that cannot legally be excluded.

Changes to These Terms

We may update these Terms as Work2Cash evolves. Material changes should be published through the app, website, email, or other reasonable notice. Continued use after an update means you accept the updated Terms.

Governing Law and Contact

These Terms are intended to be governed by applicable laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, subject to legal review before launch. For support, contact support@work2cash.ng. For privacy requests, contact privacy@work2cash.ng.

Purpose

This Tasker Agreement applies to users who create a Tasker profile, complete KYC, accept tasks, perform tasks, communicate with Task Owners, upload completion proof, receive earnings, or request withdrawals on Work2Cash.

Independent Service Provider

Taskers are independent service providers. Work2Cash does not employ Taskers, guarantee fixed income, control Tasker working hours, or provide employment benefits. Taskers are responsible for their own conduct, availability, tools, transport, taxes, and compliance with applicable law.

Tasker Activation

A user cannot accept tasks until the Tasker profile is complete, KYC is submitted and approved, required profile details are provided, relevant task categories are selected, and any platform activation requirements are satisfied.

KYC and Ongoing Review

Taskers must provide accurate identity information and may be required to complete NIN, BVN, document, selfie, biometric, or manual verification through Smile ID or another approved provider. Location and Availability

Taskers must be in Nigeria to accept tasks. A Tasker may set availability and travel radius, but must not accept tasks they cannot reasonably reach on or before the Task Owner's required arrival time. Location permission may be required while available, accepting, en route, arrived, or in progress.

Task Review Before Acceptance

Required Status Updates

Tasker status updates must be done in the app. Tap Start Journey when you begin traveling to the task site. Tap I have arrived after reaching the task site. Tap Begin Task when the task actually starts. These actions are used for live tracking, Task Owner visibility, cancellation rules, no-show review, and dispute evidence.

Work Standard

Taskers must perform accepted tasks professionally, safely, lawfully, and in line with the task description. Taskers must not misrepresent arrival, begin work before the correct state, abandon tasks without valid reason, damage property, threaten users, or demand payment outside Work2Cash.

Proof of Work Done

After task execution, the Tasker must upload proof of work done where required. Completion proof must be accurate, relevant, and respectful of privacy. Fake, copied, unrelated, unsafe, or misleading proof may lead to rejection, dispute, warning, strike, payout hold, suspension, or account removal.

Communication

Taskers must use approved Work2Cash communication channels such as task chat, voice messages, masked calls, and support channels. Real phone numbers should not be exchanged where Work2Cash provides masked calls. Requests to move payment or booking outside Work2Cash are prohibited.

Cancellations and No-Shows

Cancelling after acceptance may result in a warning unless a valid safety or emergency reason is reported and accepted. No-shows may result in a strike. Repeated cancellations, late arrival, false status updates, or poor conduct may reduce trust, restrict task access, or lead to suspension.

Unsafe Tasks

A Tasker may reject unsafe tasks without penalty. If a task becomes unsafe after acceptance, the Tasker should stop when safe, report the issue immediately through Work2Cash, provide evidence where possible, and wait for support or admin review.

Earnings and Commission

Tasker earnings are calculated after platform commission and applicable rules. Completed task count for commission tiers includes successfully completed and settled tasks only. Cancelled, refunded, unresolved dispute, or test tasks do not count.

Withdrawal Requests and Payout Batches

The minimum Tasker withdrawal is ₦1,000. Eligible withdrawal requests are processed through payout batches on the 14th and 28th of every month where supported by Paystack, Moniepoint, or another approved rail. Requests with active disputes, active issues, risk flags, invalid bank details, or provider failures may be held or retried.

Taskers are responsible for taxes, levies, bank-account accuracy, and any legal obligations arising from their earnings. Work2Cash is not responsible for loss caused by incorrect payout details supplied by a Tasker.

Ratings, Favorites, and Direct Offers

Task Owners may rate Taskers after completed tasks and may add a Tasker to favorites. A favorite Tasker may receive direct offers through Work2Cash. Direct offers still require platform rules, payment/escrow, acceptance, and task-state controls.

Warnings, Strikes, and Restrictions

Three warnings convert to one strike. Tasker Strike 1 restricts task acceptance for 2 days; Strike 2 restricts for 1 week; Strike 3 restricts for 1 month; Strike 4 may suspend the account. Work2Cash may act faster for severe safety, fraud, abuse, or legal risk.

Agreement Updates

Work2Cash may update this Tasker Agreement. Continued Tasker activity after an update means you accept the updated agreement.

Purpose

This policy explains how Work2Cash handles cancellations, no-shows, refund requests, settlement holds, warnings, strikes, and admin dispute outcomes. It applies to Task Owners and Taskers.

Escrow Principle

Task Owner payment is held in escrow before matching or direct offer acceptance. Escrow is released, refunded, partially refunded, or held based on task state, evidence, cancellation timing, completion confirmation, dispute outcome, or admin resolution.

Cancellation Rules

The current MVP cancellation rules are: Task Owner cancellation before Tasker acceptance has no penalty. Task Owner cancellation after Tasker acceptance has no penalty. Task Owner cancellation while Tasker is en route has a 10% penalty and 1 warning. Task Owner cancellation after Tasker arrives has a 10% penalty and 1 warning. Tasker cancellation after accepting gives 1 warning. Tasker no-show gives 1 strike. Task Owner no-show gives a 10% penalty and 1 warning.

Task Owner Rejection Window

A Task Owner may reject a Tasker before the Tasker starts moving and within 5 minutes after the Tasker marks en route. Rejections after movement are logged. Repeated movement-stage rejections can trigger warning, trust score reduction, or risk review.

Late Arrival and Grace

The Task Owner sets the required arrival time. The Tasker must confirm they can arrive on or before that time. A recommended grace period of 10 minutes may apply after the agreed arrival time before late/no-show reporting, subject to product settings and admin review.

Clear vs Contested Violations

Clear policy violations may be penalized immediately. Contested cases may be held for admin review or timeout. Work2Cash may request chat records, task proof, location events, status timestamps, payment records, and other evidence before deciding.

Refund Outcomes

A refund may be full, partial, denied, or converted into wallet credit depending on payment status, cancellation stage, task state, evidence, dispute outcome, payment provider status, and applicable fees. Refunds normally return to the Task Owner wallet or original payment route where supported.

Excess Task Owner Funds

Task Owner wallet funds are expected to be used for tasks. If a Task Owner deposits more than required or needs help with excess funds, they must contact support for review. Direct self-withdrawal from Task Owner wallet is not available by default.

Tasker Settlement Holds

Tasker payout delay applies only when a Tasker has one or more active issue/dispute tasks, risk flags, payout review issues, or invalid payout details. Non-disputed Tasker earnings should settle normally into the Tasker wallet. Disputed Tasker payout requires admin authorization before release.

Payout Calendar Impact

Even after earnings are settled into the Tasker wallet, withdrawal requests are paid in bulk payout batches on the 14th and 28th of each month. If a payout date falls on a non-working day or provider outage, payment may move to the next operational processing window.

Warnings and Strikes

Three warnings convert to one strike. Tasker Strike 1 restricts task acceptance for 2 days, Strike 2 for 1 week, Strike 3 for 1 month, and Strike 4 may suspend the account. Task Owner strikes reduce trust score for Strikes 1-3, while Strike 4 may suspend the account and require support review.

Admin Resolution

Where a task is disputed, Work2Cash may resolve in favor of the Task Owner, in favor of the Tasker, partially refund, fully refund, release escrow, apply penalties, warn, strike, suspend, or dismiss the report. Admin actions should be evidence-based and audit logged.

Provider Delays and Failed Payments

Payment and refund timing may depend on Paystack, Moniepoint, banks, card networks, transfer rails, webhook delays, reconciliation, fraud checks, or provider outages. Work2Cash will use reasonable efforts to reconcile failed or pending payments.

No Off-Platform Refunds

Users must not arrange refunds, compensation, or extra payments outside Work2Cash for Work2Cash tasks. Off-platform settlement can remove platform protections and may lead to warning, strike, restriction, or suspension.

Our Safety Standard

Work2Cash is built for lawful, respectful, evidence-backed task work. Every user must protect personal safety, property, privacy, and platform trust.

Respectful Conduct

Users must not harass, threaten, insult, discriminate, stalk, intimidate, extort, sexually harass, or abuse another user, admin, support agent, or third party. Unsafe behavior should be reported immediately.

Prohibited Tasks

Work2Cash does not allow illegal activities, adult or sexual services, weapons-related tasks, intimidation or violence, drug-related tasks, high-risk medical tasks, licensed professional tasks unless specifically approved, overnight stay tasks, tasks involving minors without appropriate guardian presence, unsafe travel or access, tasks outside approved categories, or any task that violates law or public safety.

Off-Platform Requests

Users must not request, encourage, or accept off-platform payment, direct booking, phone-number exchange for bypassing Work2Cash, or any arrangement intended to avoid platform escrow, fees, safety controls, or dispute records. Off-platform solicitation is reportable.

Task Site Safety

Task Owners must provide a safe and lawful task environment. The task site should be accessible, accurately described, and free from avoidable hazards. Taskers should leave or refuse if a situation becomes unsafe and should report the issue through Work2Cash.

Tasker Safety Rights

Taskers may reject unsafe tasks without penalty. After acceptance, a Tasker may cancel without penalty only if a valid safety issue is reported and accepted through review. Evidence such as messages, photos, location, or status timestamps may be requested.

Privacy and Media

Users must not upload private, illegal, misleading, unrelated, or humiliating media. Task proof should show the work needed or work completed, not expose unnecessary personal information. Admin may remove unsafe or invalid media and log the reason.

Communication Safety

Chat, voice messages, masked calls, and support communication must be used only for task-related and support-related purposes. Work2Cash may review communication metadata, reports, and evidence for safety, fraud, support, or dispute reasons. Voice messages are treated as user-generated evidence and should follow the same safety rules.

Masked Calls

Masked calls are provided to protect real phone numbers where technically and operationally available. Users must not pressure others to reveal real phone numbers. Before calls, users should see a reminder to keep task communication and payment inside Work2Cash.

Emergency Situations

Work2Cash support is not a police, ambulance, fire, or emergency-response service. If there is immediate danger, users should contact local emergency services or nearby authorities first, then report the incident to Work2Cash when safe.

Reports and Enforcement

Users can report no-show, poor service, unsafe behavior, harassment, damage/theft, off-platform request, wrong location, payment/refund issue, and other concerns. Verified abuse may lead to warnings, strikes, trust-score changes, settlement holds, suspension, reporting to authorities, or permanent removal.

Blocking Users

Task Owners and Taskers may block each other after a task or support case where the feature is available. Blocking prevents future direct offers or preferred matching but does not delete task history, evidence, finance records, or audit records.

Why Location Is Needed

Work2Cash uses location to create accurate task sites, help Taskers find nearby work, sort tasks nearest first, calculate ETA, support live task tracking, verify arrival, support safety review, and resolve disputes.

Task Owner Location Use

A Task Owner may use live location or manually enter a task site address. When an address is manually entered, Work2Cash may geocode it into coordinates and ask the Task Owner to confirm the map pin before posting. A Task Owner can be anywhere, but the task site must be in Nigeria.

Tasker Location Use

A Tasker must be in Nigeria to accept tasks. Location may be required while the Tasker is available, browsing tasks, filtering tasks by location, accepting a task, starting journey, travelling to the task site, marking arrival, beginning the task, working, or resolving an active task issue.

Live Tracking Window

Live tracking begins when a task has been accepted and the Tasker is en route or otherwise in a valid active task state. Tracking should stop when the task is completed, cancelled, expired, disputed without live tracking need, or otherwise no longer requires location updates.

ETA Updates

Work2Cash may calculate ETA using a map provider such as Google Maps. To reduce cost, battery use, and unnecessary provider calls, paid ETA refresh may be guarded. The architecture uses a 5-minute timer plus the next 10% total-journey progress milestone, with journey progress being the main trigger. After a refresh, the timer and milestone reset.

Accuracy

If GPS accuracy is poor, Work2Cash may warn the user, retry location capture, or request confirmation. Users must not falsify location, mark arrival before arriving, or use tools to manipulate task tracking.

Address Privacy

Public task listings show limited location information. Exact task address and full proof media are not meant to be public. They are shown only when platform rules are satisfied, such as payment, acceptance, and valid task state.

Location in Disputes

Location records, geocoding records, arrival timestamps, journey events, and task state changes may be used to review late arrival, no-show, wrong location, unsafe location, cancellation, and completion disputes.

Turning Off Location

You may control location permission through your device settings. Some Work2Cash features may not work without location permission, especially Tasker availability, task acceptance, journey tracking, arrival confirmation, and location-based discovery.

Contact

For questions about location data, contact privacy@work2cash.ng or support@work2cash.ng.

Payment Providers

Work2Cash may use Paystack, Moniepoint, banks, card networks, transfer processors, and other approved providers to collect payments, verify transactions, hold references, process refunds, and transfer eligible Tasker payouts.

Escrow Funding

Task Owners must fund tasks before matching, public discovery acceptance, or direct favorite Tasker offer completion. Funds are held in escrow and released or refunded according to task state, completion, cancellation, dispute, and admin resolution rules.

Backend Verification

A payment is not treated as successful only because a user saw a redirect, uploaded a screenshot, or tapped a button. Work2Cash relies on backend verification, provider webhooks, provider references, and reconciliation to confirm payment status.

Wallet Types

A user may have a Task Owner wallet and a Tasker wallet. The Task Owner wallet is for task funding, refunds, and credits. The Tasker wallet receives task earnings and supports withdrawal requests subject to eligibility and policy checks.

Task Owner Wallet Restrictions

Task Owner wallet funds are expected to be used for tasks. Direct self-withdrawal from the Task Owner wallet is not available by default. If excess funds need review, the Task Owner must contact support.

Tasker Earnings

Tasker earnings are credited after task completion, escrow release, and applicable commission/fee rules. Tasker earnings display should show gross task amount, platform commission, and net earning where applicable.

Commission Tiers

The default platform commission is 20%. The rate may reduce to 17.5% after 20 completed tasks, 15% after 50 completed tasks, 12.5% after 100 completed tasks, and 10% after 500 completed tasks. Only successfully completed and settled tasks count toward tier progression.

Minimum Amounts

The minimum task amount is ₦2,000. The minimum Tasker withdrawal is ₦1,000. Work2Cash may update minimums after notice or as required for provider cost, fraud prevention, or marketplace health.

Withdrawal Requests

Submitting a withdrawal request does not guarantee immediate payment. Work2Cash may review withdrawal eligibility, KYC, bank details, risk flags, active disputes, settlement holds, provider limits, and payout calendar before payment.

Payout Batch Dates

Eligible Tasker withdrawal requests are paid in bulk batches on the 14th and 28th of every month using Paystack, Moniepoint, or other approved payout rails where available. Requests submitted too close to a batch date, requests under review, failed provider transfers, or held payouts may move to the next batch.

Settlement Holds

Work2Cash may hold settlement or payout for active disputes, active issues, suspected fraud, chargebacks, reversed payments, duplicate provider events, invalid proof, unsafe conduct, KYC concerns, or admin review. Non-disputed earnings should not be held unnecessarily.

Refunds

Refunds may be issued to the Task Owner wallet or original payment route where supported. Payment-processing fees, provider fees, cancellation penalties, and partial service outcomes may affect refund amount. Provider timing can affect when funds become visible.

Failed, Pending, or Duplicate Payments

If a payment is failed, pending, duplicated, or unclear, Work2Cash may reconcile with Paystack, Moniepoint, or other providers before updating wallet or task state. Users should not create duplicate payments unless the app instructs them to do so.

Chargebacks and Reversals

Chargebacks, bank reversals, fraudulent payments, or provider reversals may cause escrow holds, wallet debit, task restriction, dispute review, risk flags, suspension, or recovery actions.

Bank Details and Transfer Errors

Taskers are responsible for accurate payout bank details. Work2Cash is not responsible for losses caused by incorrect details supplied by a user, except where Work2Cash caused the error. Failed transfers may be retried, reversed, or held for support review.

Ledger Records

Wallet balances must be backed by ledger records. Work2Cash may maintain transaction history, escrow records, payout batch records, webhook logs, admin audit logs, and reconciliation logs for finance, safety, audit, and legal purposes.

Provider Terms

Payment providers may have their own terms, limits, downtime, bank-transfer charges, card fees, settlement timelines, and compliance checks. Users may be subject to provider terms when using payment or payout services.
